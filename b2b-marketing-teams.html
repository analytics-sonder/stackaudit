The problem

Four ways B2B marketing teams silently overspend

Unused seat licenses

You bought 15 seats. 7 people actively use the tool. You're paying for 8 ghost seats every month. Nobody noticed because renewal auto-fired. StackAudit flags this with the exact reduction and saving.

Duplicate tools, same job

Apollo, ZoomInfo, and Lusha all do prospecting. Canva and Adobe Express both do social creatives. Every category with overlap is money leaking quietly. StackAudit maps it and shows which to benchmark.

Wrong plan tier

You're on Enterprise because someone clicked upgrade 18 months ago. Your team uses 20% of its features. StackAudit scores your plan justification and flags every tool where you're overbuying.

Renewals that auto-fire

The contract renews. Nobody reviewed it. You're locked in for another year at last year's headcount. StackAudit sends renewal alerts 25–60 days before each tool renews, so you have time to act.

FAQ

Common questions from marketing teams

How do B2B marketing teams waste money on SaaS tools?
The most common sources are unused seat licenses, overlapping tools in the same category (multiple lead databases, multiple design tools), overbuilt plan tiers where you're paying for Enterprise features with Starter-level usage, monthly billing where annual would save 20%, and paid add-ons nobody uses. StackAudit detects all of these automatically.
How long does a full stack audit take?
Most teams complete their first audit in 10–15 minutes. You add each tool manually — about 60 seconds per tool — and StackAudit generates scores and recommendations instantly. No integrations, no API connections, no IT involvement needed.
What saving does StackAudit typically identify?
For a 10–50 person B2B marketing team spending $1,500–$5,000/month on tools, StackAudit typically identifies $300–$800/month in recoverable savings. The most common sources are unused seats, wrong plan tier, and duplicate tools in the lead enrichment or design categories.
Do I need to give StackAudit access to my tools?
No. StackAudit is entirely manual-input based. You enter the details (cost, seats, plan tier, usage) and the scoring engine does the rest. No integrations, no API keys, no read access to any of your tools.
